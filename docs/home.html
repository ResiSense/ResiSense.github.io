According to the World Health Organisation,
antibiotic resistance contributes to

5 MILLION
DEATHS

per year globally

Notably,
4.3 MILLION

of which are occurring in
Low- and Middle-Income Countries (LMICs)

Antibiotics have been used since the 1940s to treat bacterial infections
and they are still among our most successful methods of medical intervention

However, antibiotic resistance has proliferated due to antibiotic misuse
causing previously manageable diseases to resurface

MISUSE OF ANTIBIOTICS
such as incomplete or improper antibiotic treatment

aggravates antimicrobial resistance development

Antibiotic-resistant strains form by
random mutation or horizontal gene transfer

Antibiotics kill sensitive bacteria
but the resistant ones survive

Without competition,
resistant strains proliferate rapidly

Detection of antibiotic resistance
is critical for controlling this SILENT PANDEMIC!

Current technology
for detecting antibiotic resistance
in low- and middle-income countries includes...

Minimum Inhibitory

Concentration (MIC) Tests

Requires length culturing:
Time-consuming

Genetic Tests (PCR)

Requires trained personnel:
Low accessibility

A FASTER, CHEAPER, and SIMPLER
technology is needed!

Introducing our solution...

A Cell-Free Detection System
for Antibiotic Resistance Genes

ResiSense provides
a point-of-care antibiotic test that can...

help doctors choose the
proper antibiotics
to prescribe

prevent the spread of
antibiotic resistance by
alerting medical
professionals
of its existence

provide a
high accessibility
to tests for places that lack access
to traditional antibiotic tests
